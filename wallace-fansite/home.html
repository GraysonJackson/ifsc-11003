Behold: the legend, the scholar, the protein-powered cybersecurity prodigy himself — Carter Wallace

Carter Wallace is what happens when discipline meets chaos and decides to double major. As a Cybersecurity and Computer Science student at UA Little Rock, Carter spends his days locking down digital fortresses and his nights destroying enemies in CSGO. He's a Donaghey Scholar, a ransomware researcher, and the proud owner of The Swagger Wagon — a vintage Toyota minivan that's more iconic than any celebrity car could ever dream to be.

While others rely on caffeine, Carter powers himself with pure protein and unshakable determination. You'll find him sipping a protein shake like it's fine wine, probably explaining why two-factor authentication is the key to civilization's survival.

But don't let his academic accolades fool you — Carter's also the life of the party. He's energetic, hilarious, and has never once been spotted without at least one witty comment locked and loaded. Carter isn't just a student — he's an experience.

  • Once ran on nothing but protein and spite during finals week.
  • Refuses to drink caffeine. Like, morally.
  • Uses the gamertag “Mountain” — a name that symbolizes both his gaming dominance and his peak-level chill (except during CSGO ranked matches).
  • Former YouTube sensation (a.k.a. iMountain), known to at least seven loyal fans and one confused algorithm.
“You can’t ransomware my heart.” Probably Carter
